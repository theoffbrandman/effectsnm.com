Past Projects

Alameda - Quinn Ennis - 1/23/2022

Most recent experiment: Students planted their own lettuce plants and grew them in a hydroponic system. This process allowed students to learn about the requirements for plant growth. Using the scientific method, students designed their own experiments to measure the effects of a growth media on root and leaf development. At the end of the project, students harvested and ate the lettuce while presenting their results. Throughout the experiment, students tracked three variables of their choosing: plant height, number of leaves, and leaf color. Students were also introduced to the ideas of pH and rate of growth during the project.



Hawthorne - Neil Mahto - 1/23/2022

Recently, students have been learning about forms of energy and aerodynamics. Specifically, they learned about potential, kinetic, and elastic energy. They also learned about drag, lift, thrust, and gravity as it relates to aerodynamics. Using those two concepts, students were able to construct catapults and paper airplanes. After a few trials, students learned what might be holding their results back and made adjustments based on the scientific principles they had been taught.
